# Social Proof

People trust other people more than they trust your marketing copy.

## How to use

- `/social-proof` Add or improve social proof on any page in this conversation.

## Constraints

### Types of Proof (Strongest to Weakest)
1. **Specific numbers:** "12,000 teams use [product]" or "Processed $4M in invoices this month." Concrete beats vague.
2. **Named testimonials with photos:** Real name, real photo, real company. "Sarah K., Head of Ops at Retool" beats "S.K., Marketing."
3. **Company logos:** Show logos of recognizable customers. 4-6 logos in a row. No more than 8.
4. **Screenshots of real usage:** Show actual tweets, Slack messages, or reviews where people praise the product.
5. **Ratings/awards:** "4.8 on G2" or "Product Hunt #1" with the actual badge.
6. **Generic testimonials:** "Great product!" from "John D." This is almost worthless. Avoid it.

### Placement
- MUST place social proof near the CTA. When someone is about to decide, that's when they need reassurance.
- Logo bar works directly below the hero section. "Trusted by" + 4-6 logos.
- Testimonials work between feature sections. Break up your own claims with someone else validating them.
- Numbers work in the hero itself. "Join 12,000+ teams" as a line above or below the CTA.

### Making Proof Believable
- MUST use specifics. "Saved 12 hours per week" beats "Saved time." A number with a unit is 3x more credible than a vague claim.
- MUST use real photos, not stock photos. People can tell. If you don't have photos, use company logos instead.
- MUST attribute to real people with real titles at real companies. Anonymous proof is barely proof.
- SHOULD include the result, not just the praise. "We cut onboarding time by 40%" is proof. "Love this tool!" is noise.

### Anti-Patterns
- Fake testimonials (people can sense them and they destroy trust)
- "As seen in TechCrunch" when you had a passing mention in a roundup
- Too many logos (8+ feels like padding, 4-6 feels curated)
- Testimonials that praise the product without naming a specific benefit
- Social proof section buried at the bottom of the page where nobody scrolls
