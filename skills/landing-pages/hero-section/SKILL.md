# Hero Section

5 seconds. That's how long you have to make someone care.

## How to use

- `/hero-section` Evaluate and improve a hero section in this conversation.

## Constraints

### The Formula
- MUST contain exactly these elements:
  1. **Headline:** What you do, for whom, in under 10 words. Not your company mission. Not a clever pun. The value.
  2. **Subhead:** One sentence expanding on the headline. What makes it different or how it works.
  3. **CTA:** One button. One action. "Get Started," "Try Free," "See How It Works." Not two buttons competing.
  4. **Visual proof:** Screenshot, demo, or video showing the actual product. Not a stock photo. Not an abstract illustration.
- That's it. Four elements. Everything else is a distraction.

### Headline Rules
- MUST be specific. "The project management tool for remote teams" beats "Reimagining how teams work together."
- MUST pass the "so what" test. Read the headline. If someone can say "so what?" you're too vague.
- MUST name the outcome or the audience. "Send invoices in 30 seconds" (outcome). "The CRM for indie consultants" (audience).
- NEVER start with "Welcome to [product name]." Nobody cares about your product name yet.
- SHOULD be under 10 words. Every extra word dilutes the impact.

### Visual Proof
- MUST show the actual product. Real screenshots with real data (or realistic demo data). Not mockups in fancy device frames. Not illustrations of abstract concepts.
- A product screenshot communicates more value in 1 second than a paragraph of text communicates in 30 seconds.
- SHOULD show the product doing the thing your headline promises.

### Anti-Patterns
- Abstract hero illustrations that don't show the product
- Two or more CTAs competing ("Sign Up" and "Book Demo" and "Learn More" all at once)
- Headlines that describe the category instead of the value ("AI-Powered Automation Platform")
- Carousels or auto-rotating hero content (nobody waits for slide 3)
- Giant hero sections with no product screenshot
