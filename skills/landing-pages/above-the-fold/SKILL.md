# Above the Fold

The first viewport decides whether anyone sees the rest of your page.

## How to use

- `/above-the-fold` Evaluate and optimize the first viewport of any page in this conversation.

## Constraints

### What Must Be Above the Fold
- MUST include: headline, subhead, primary CTA, and visual proof (product screenshot or demo)
- SHOULD include: one line of social proof ("Trusted by 5,000+ teams" or a logo bar)
- Navigation must be present but quiet. It shouldn't compete with the headline.
- MUST answer in 5 seconds: What is this? Who is it for? What should I do next?

### What Must NOT Be Above the Fold
- Your company story or mission statement
- A wall of feature bullet points
- Multiple CTAs competing for attention
- An auto-playing video that blocks the content
- A cookie banner that covers 40% of the viewport (design it to be minimal)

### The 5-Second Test
- Show someone your above-the-fold for 5 seconds. Then hide it. Ask them: what does this product do? Who is it for? What would you click?
- If they can't answer all three, your above-the-fold is failing.
- MUST prioritize clarity over cleverness. A boring headline that communicates clearly converts better than a clever headline that confuses.

### Mobile Above the Fold
- Mobile viewport is ~667px tall. Your hero needs to fit.
- MUST ensure the CTA is visible without scrolling on mobile. If the user has to scroll past a giant hero image to find the button, you've lost them.
- SHOULD reduce the hero image size on mobile or move it below the CTA.
- Navigation should collapse to a hamburger. Don't waste mobile fold space on 6 nav links.

### Anti-Patterns
- Giant hero images that push the CTA below the fold
- Animated elements that delay content visibility
- "Scroll down to learn more" arrows (if you need to tell people to scroll, your fold content isn't working)
- Full-screen splash pages with no content until you interact
- Navigation that takes up 100px+ of vertical space on mobile
