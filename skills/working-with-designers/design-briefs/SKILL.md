# Design Briefs

A 30-minute brief saves 30 hours of revision. Write it before anyone opens Figma.

## How to use

- `/design-briefs` Write or improve a design brief in this conversation.

## Constraints

### The Brief Template
- MUST include these sections:

**1. What we're building (2-3 sentences)**
- The specific deliverable. "A pricing page with three tiers" not "a pricing experience."

**2. Who it's for (2-3 sentences)**
- The actual user. Their technical level, what they care about, what they're comparing you against. "Solo founders using AI tools to build products. Most are technical but not designers. They compare us against competitors who look more polished."

**3. What success looks like (1-2 sentences)**
- The measurable outcome. "Users can compare plans and start a trial within 30 seconds" or "Bounce rate drops below 40%."

**4. Constraints**
- Technical: "Must work in React/Next.js. Must be responsive."
- Brand: "Use Inter font. Primary color #2563eb. Tone is professional but approachable."
- Scope: "Desktop and mobile. No animations for v1."
- Timeline: "Need first draft by Friday."

**5. References (3-5 links)**
- Products to draw from, with specific notes. "Stripe's pricing page — the clarity of plan comparison. Linear's marketing site — the typography and density. Plausible's dashboard — the simplicity."
- MUST say what to take from each reference. Just linking 5 sites without context is useless.

**6. What this is NOT**
- Explicit boundaries. "This is not a redesign of the entire product. This is not a brand exercise. Don't spend time on logo or illustrations."

### Brief Quality Signals
- Specific enough that two different designers would produce recognizably similar solutions
- Short enough to read in 5 minutes
- Includes references with specific callouts
- Names the user and their context
- Has clear scope boundaries

### Anti-Patterns
- Briefs that describe the solution instead of the problem ("make a blue header with a dropdown menu" is a spec, not a brief)
- No references (forces the designer to guess your quality bar)
- Briefs that describe everything except what success looks like
- 10-page briefs that nobody reads (keep it to 1 page)
- Briefs with no constraints (unlimited scope = unlimited revisions)
