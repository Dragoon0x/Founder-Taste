# Giving Feedback

"I don't like it" is not feedback. "Make it pop" is not feedback. Here's actual feedback.

## How to use

- `/giving-feedback` Structure design feedback in this conversation.

## Constraints

### The Feedback Structure
- MUST follow: **What I see → Why it concerns me → Question**
- "I see two buttons that look equally important (observation). I'm worried users won't know which one to click first (concern). Could we make one clearly primary? (question)"
- This structure is honest, specific, and doesn't prescribe a solution. Designers can work with it.

### What to Say
- Describe the problem, not the solution. "Users might not notice the CTA" is better than "make the button bigger and red."
- Reference user behavior. "A first-time user landing here wouldn't know what to do" is better than "this doesn't look good."
- Name the priority. "The most important thing on this screen should be X, but right now Y is getting more attention."
- Ask questions instead of giving commands. "What happens if we simplify this to just the top 3 options?" is better than "remove half of these options."

### What Not to Say
- "I don't like it" (why? be specific)
- "Make it pop" (this means nothing)
- "Can we try something more modern?" (modern is not a direction)
- "My wife/friend/investor thinks..." (feedback by proxy is unreliable)
- "Can you just move this here and change that to blue?" (you're designing now, let them design)

### Founder Feedback Rights
- You have the right to say: "This isn't communicating our value. Users need to understand X within 5 seconds."
- You have the right to say: "The most important action on this page is Y and it should be the most prominent thing."
- You have the right to say: "This doesn't feel like our brand. We're [approachable/professional/technical], and this reads as [cold/playful/generic]."
- You do NOT have the right to pixel-push unless you're also the designer. State the problem. Let them solve it.

### Anti-Patterns
- Giving feedback on aesthetics before the structure and hierarchy are reviewed
- Providing the solution instead of describing the problem
- Rewriting copy in the feedback instead of explaining what the copy should accomplish
- Comparing to a competitor without explaining what specifically to take from them
- Giving feedback on everything at once instead of prioritizing the top 3 issues
