# Screenshot Test

Screenshot your product. Put it next to a product you admire. The gaps become obvious.

## How to use

- `/screenshot-test` Run a screenshot comparison test on any product in this conversation.

## Constraints

### The Comparison Method
- MUST screenshot your product and place it side-by-side with a product you consider high quality in the same category.
- MUST compare the same type of page: your dashboard vs. their dashboard, your pricing vs. their pricing.
- MUST look at these five things specifically:
  1. **Spacing:** is their spacing more consistent? More generous?
  2. **Typography:** do they have clearer size differences between headings and body?
  3. **Color:** are they using fewer colors more intentionally?
  4. **Density:** are they showing less per screen with better hierarchy?
  5. **Polish:** do their buttons, inputs, and cards feel more refined?

### The Squint Test
- Blur your eyes or step 3 feet from the screen. Look at both screenshots.
- Can you tell what's most important on their page? Can you tell on yours?
- If their hierarchy reads clearly at a squint and yours doesn't, you have a hierarchy problem.

### The Thumbnail Test
- Shrink both screenshots to thumbnail size (200px wide).
- At thumbnail, only the largest shapes and strongest contrasts survive.
- If your thumbnail looks like a gray blob with no clear structure, your visual hierarchy is too flat.

### The Phone Test
- Pull your product up on your phone. Use it for 2 minutes.
- Every tap that requires aiming = the target is too small.
- Every time you squint = the text is too small or low contrast.
- Every time you get lost = the navigation is broken.
- MUST fix every issue you found in 2 minutes of phone usage before worrying about anything else.

### Anti-Patterns
- Comparing against the best product in the world and feeling defeated (compare against products at your stage)
- Only comparing visual style when the real problem is structure and hierarchy
- Running the test once and never again (run it monthly as the product evolves)
- Ignoring the comparison results because "our audience is different"
