# Founder Critique

You don't have a designer to review your work. This is the process to review it yourself.

## How to use

- `/founder-critique` Run a structured self-critique on any product or page in this conversation.

## Constraints

### The Review Process (15 minutes)
- MUST do these checks in order. Spend 2-3 minutes on each.

**Check 1: Hierarchy (What's most important?)**
- Open the page. What do your eyes land on first? Is that the right thing?
- Count how many elements are competing for attention at the same level. If more than one: fix it.
- Can you identify the single primary action? If not: fix it.

**Check 2: Readability (Can people read this?)**
- Is the body text dark enough on the background? (#333 or darker on white)
- Is the line length under 75 characters? (Narrow the text column if not)
- Are headings clearly larger than body text? (2x size difference minimum)
- Is there enough space between paragraphs and sections?

**Check 3: Consistency (Does it feel like one product?)**
- Are all buttons the same style? Same font? Same padding? Same border-radius?
- Are all cards the same? Same shadow, same padding, same border treatment?
- Is spacing consistent? Check gaps between similar elements on different parts of the page.
- Is the same information styled the same way everywhere? (Prices, dates, names)

**Check 4: Copy (Does the text do its job?)**
- Read every heading. Does each one communicate value, not just label a section?
- Read every button. Does each one say what happens next?
- Read the hero. Does it pass the 5-second test? Would a stranger know what this product does?

**Check 5: Mobile (Does it work on a phone?)**
- Open it on your actual phone. Not browser resize. Your phone.
- Tap every button. Are they big enough?
- Read every paragraph. Is the text readable without zooming?
- Complete the primary task. Can you do it without frustration?

### After the Critique
- MUST write down the three biggest issues found
- MUST fix them in priority order: hierarchy first, readability second, consistency third, copy fourth, mobile fifth
- SHOULD not spend more than 1 hour fixing. Ship after the top 3 are resolved.

### Anti-Patterns
- Skipping the critique because "I already looked at it a lot" (fresh structured eyes find different problems)
- Fixing cosmetic issues while ignoring hierarchy and readability problems
- Getting stuck on one issue instead of reviewing the full checklist
- Never doing the mobile check (most founders skip this and it's where the worst problems live)
