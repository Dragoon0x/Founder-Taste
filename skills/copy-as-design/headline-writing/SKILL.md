# Headline Writing

Your headline is doing 80% of your conversion work. Treat it like it matters.

## How to use

- `/headline-writing` Write or improve headlines for any page in this conversation.

## Constraints

### The Formula
- MUST follow one of these structures:
  - **Outcome:** what the user gets. "Send invoices in 30 seconds." "Know your metrics before your morning coffee."
  - **Audience + outcome:** who it's for + what they get. "The CRM for indie consultants." "Analytics for teams that ship fast."
  - **Problem → solution:** state the pain, offer the fix. "Stop chasing payments. Get paid on time, every time."
- MUST be under 10 words. Every word over 10 dilutes impact. Force yourself to cut.
- MUST be specific. Replace generic words with concrete ones. "Improve your workflow" → "Cut meeting prep from 2 hours to 5 minutes."

### The Tests
- **So-what test:** read your headline out loud. If someone can respond "so what?" it's too vague.
- **Swap test:** could a competitor use this exact headline? If yes, it's too generic. "The Modern Way to Manage Projects" could be anyone. "Track every bug from report to fix in one screen" can't.
- **Parent test:** would your parent understand what the product does from the headline alone? If not, simplify.

### Section Headlines
- Feature sections need headlines too. Not "Features" — that's a label, not a headline.
- Each feature section headline should state the benefit: "See all your leads in one place" not "Lead Management Dashboard."
- SHOULD follow a pattern across sections. If your hero is outcome-focused, keep section headlines outcome-focused.

### Anti-Patterns
- Starting with the product name ("Acme helps teams collaborate better")
- Category descriptions instead of value ("AI-Powered Project Management")
- Buzzword soup ("Leverage next-gen AI to optimize your workflow paradigms")
- Questions as headlines on landing pages ("Ready to transform your business?" — nobody answers yes to a stranger's question)
- Headlines that require reading the subhead to make sense
