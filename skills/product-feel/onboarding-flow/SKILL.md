# Onboarding Flow

The goal is not to teach users your product. The goal is to get them to the moment where they think "oh, this is useful."

## How to use

- `/onboarding-flow` Design or improve an onboarding flow in this conversation.

## Constraints

### The Core Principle
- MUST get users to value in under 2 minutes. Not "complete setup." Value. The moment they see why this product exists for them.
- MUST ask for the minimum information needed to deliver value. Name + email to start. Everything else can come later.
- MUST show the product doing something useful within the first 60 seconds. Not a product tour. Not a feature walkthrough. Actual value.
- NEVER start with a 5-step setup wizard that asks for company size, role, industry, and preferences before showing anything.

### What to Ask, When
- Step 1: the absolute minimum to create an account (email, maybe name)
- Step 2: the one piece of context that makes the product useful ("What's the name of your project?" or "Connect your data source")
- Step 3: show value immediately with that context
- Everything else: ask later, progressively, when the information is needed

### The Empty Product Problem
- When users first log in, the product is empty. Empty products feel broken.
- MUST populate with sample data, templates, or a guided first task. Never show a blank dashboard on first login.
- "Create your first [thing]" with one big CTA is better than showing an empty list and hoping users figure it out.
- Templates work: "Start with a template" reduces the blank-page problem and shows users what's possible.

### Progress and Momentum
- SHOULD show progress (step 1 of 3) for multi-step onboarding. Keep it under 4 steps.
- MUST let users skip non-essential steps. If they want to explore first, let them.
- SHOULD celebrate the first meaningful action ("Your first invoice is ready!" or "Dashboard created") with clear next steps.

### Anti-Patterns
- Product tours that point at UI elements nobody asked about
- Asking for information you don't need yet (billing during signup for a free tier)
- Onboarding that requires reading instructions instead of doing things
- No onboarding at all (just dropping users into a complex product)
- Forced tutorials that can't be dismissed
